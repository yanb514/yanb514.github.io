I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor in the School of Sustainable Engineering and the Built Environment (SSEBE) at Arizona State University. Before this, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Transportation and Power Systems (TAPS) division at Argonne National Laboratory. I earned my Ph.D. from the Institute for Software Integrated Systems at Vanderbilt University under the guidance of Professor Dan Work, where I also served as a Research Engineer.

I am a five-time recipient of the prestigious Dwight D. Eisenhower Transportation Fellowship awarded by the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) and was recognized as a Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) Rising Star by the University of Virginia Link Lab in 2023. I also held research internship positions at Toyota Infotech Labs and Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL), and was a visiting researcher at the Institute for Pure & Applied Mathematics (IPAM) at UCLA.

My research focuses on improving the efficiency and operational performance of transportation systems by leveraging advancements in large-scale sensing, computation, communication, and control technologies. I integrate computational approaches—such as simulation, estimation, and control—with real-world field deployments to bridge the gap between theory and practice.
